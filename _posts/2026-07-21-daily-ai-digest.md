### The Resurgence of Marginal Costs in AI
*   The emergence of capable Chinese open-weights models like Kimi K3 challenges the previous tech paradigm of near-zero marginal costs, bringing traditional economic principles back to the forefront.
*   A key distinction exists between fixed R&D costs (for creating the model) and variable COGS (cost of goods sold for inference), which correlates directly with revenue and is now significant for AI.
*   Tokens from different models are not fungible commodities; the true commodity is the resulting "intelligence," making token efficiency a critical factor in determining COGS and competitive advantage.
*   As AI intelligence commoditizes for many tasks, profitability will depend on superior cost structures rather than unique capabilities, following classic commodity market dynamics.
*   Currently, demand exceeds supply for frontier models, granting high margins to leaders like Anthropic and OpenAI, but this landscape may shift as competition and capacity increase.

### AI智能领域边际成本的回归
*   Kimi K3等中国开源模型的出现挑战了科技界“边际成本趋近于零”的旧范式，传统经济原则正重新成为行业讨论的焦点。
*   需要明确区分固定的研发成本（R&D）与可变的销货成本（COGS）。在AI领域，推理产生的COGS与收入直接相关，已不可忽视。
*   不同模型生成的Token并非同质化商品，真正同质化的是Token所构建的“智能”。因此，Token效率成为决定COGS和竞争力的关键。
*   随着AI智能在众多任务中趋于商品化，盈利将不再依赖于独特能力，而取决于更优越的成本结构，这符合经典的商品市场动态。
*   目前，前沿模型市场供不应求，使Anthropic和OpenAI等领导者保持高利润率，但随着竞争加剧和算力提升，市场格局可能发生变化。
